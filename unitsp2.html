Algebraic equations

working out the Algebraic equations

The lowest factors of 2000 are 2×2×2×2×5×5×5.These factors are written as 2 to power 4 ×5 to power 3, where 2 and 5 are called bases and the numbers 4 and 3 are called indices. When an index is an integer it is called a power. Thus, 2 to power 4 is called ‘two to the power of four’, and has a base of 2 and an index of 4. Similarly, 5 to power 3 is called ‘five to the power of 3’and has a base of 5 and an index of 3. Special names may be used when the indices are 2 and 3,these being called ‘squared’ and ‘cubed’,respectively.Thus 7 to power 2 is called ‘seven squared’ and 9 to power 3 is called ‘nine cubed’. When no index is shown, the power is 1, i.e. 2 means 2 to power 1.

Reciprocal
The reciprocal of a number is when the index is −1 and its value is given by 1 divided by the base. Thus the reciprocal of 2 is 2 to power −1 and its value is 1/2 or 0.5. Similarly, the reciprocal of 5 is 5 to power −1 which means 1/5 or 0.2
Square root
The square root of a number is when the index is 1/2 , and the square root of 2 is written as 2 into power (1/2) or √2. The value of a square root is the value of the base which when multiplied by itself gives the number. Since 3×3=9, then √9=3.
However, (−3)×(−3)=9, so √9=−3.
There are always two answers when finding the square root of a number and this is shown by putting both a + and a − sign in front of the answer to a square root problem.
Thus √9=±3 and 4 to power 1/2 =√4=±2, and so on.
Laws of indices
When simplifying calculations involving indices, certain basic rules or laws can be applied, called the laws of indices.
